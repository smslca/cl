The process — nothing else matters
  1. Read the weather. WEAK → smaller size or sit out. You only control what you lose.
  2. Shortlist empty → do nothing. Most days are do-nothing days.
  3. Shortlist name + PRIORITY sector agree → candidate. Buy next day, don't wait for a dip.
  4. 9:15 gate: NIFTY opens below −0.2% or above +0.5% → no entries today, whatever the list says. Tested on 982 entries — both fear and euphoria openings lose. Holdings ride; only entries stop.
  5. Size by your risk rules. Write down why you entered.
  6. Never buy the laggard because the leader is extended. Park extended leaders; re-enter when they rest.
How to read the numbers
RS — a rank, not a gain: "beat 93%" means only 7 of every 100 NSE stocks did better this month.
rvol — today's volume ÷ own 20-day average. 2x = twice the normal interest. Below 2x = louder than usual, but below the level the backtest certified.
delivery X% vs Y% — of every 100 shares traded, how many were taken home overnight (real ownership) vs bought-and-sold same day (churn). X = today, Y = the stock's own 20-day usual. X above Y = conviction buyers; X ≈ Y on a big-volume day = the extra activity was churn, loud not deep.
deliv streak — of the last 5 sessions, how many had delivery above that usual. 4/5 = someone is taking stock home all week — quiet accumulation.
ext — % above own 20-SMA. Stock >15% (sector >7%) = late; wait for it to rest.
of high — % of 52-week high (or high since listing). Near 100% = no trapped sellers overhead.
accum — share of a sector's members with delivery above their own average this week.

Mentor's notes — open when tempted
  1. Rotation is bought while it's building, not after it's on the front page. The difference between buying strength and buying someone's exit is when in the move you arrive.
  2. "Not extended" must never decay into "buy the laggard." The 4th-best stock in a hot sector is not a discount. If a priority sector has no actionable name, the answer is no trade — not scrolling down the member list.
  3. Extension is a timing statement, never a quality statement. Your backtest: the runaways were the biggest winners. Park extended leaders, don't delete them — pounce when they rest and requalify.
  4. Your instincts are hypotheses, not vetoes. The upper-wick fear and the wait-for-pullback comfort were both refuted by your own 505 days. Every "my mind says" goes to the data for trial before it touches an order.
  5. Grade measures confluence, not signal strength. An A+ with 1.8x volume is context agreeing about a whisper. Read the reason column before the grade.
  6. Among equal grades: moderate volume beats freak spikes, deep delivery beats thin, near the 20-SMA beats stretched — and two rows from one sector are one bet. These are reading rules, not a sort order; the journal decides if your picks beat taking the top row.
  7. The entry needs no finesse; the stop needs no mercy. The tested edge bought the next close, dumbly. Stop = signal-day low, exit = close below 20-SMA, size = risk ÷ stop distance. All feelings live inside the qty column, nowhere else.
  8. Tight stop means cheap to be wrong, not safe to be huge. The position cap exists because four tight-stop days would otherwise concentrate the whole account.
  9. Capital is promoted by adherence, not confidence. 20 logged trades at ≥90% process adherence doubles the stake. Confidence follows winning streaks — the exact wrong time to add money. One overridden stop resets the count.
  10. Top 1% traders don't think better — they act and journal. Frameworks are in every book recited by losing traders. Take the setup, cut without negotiating, write it down, review monthly, survive to compound. This dashboard's P&L is zero until you do.
